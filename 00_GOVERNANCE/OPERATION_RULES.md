# Operation Rules

Version: AER v1.0

Status: Frozen

---

## Purpose

This document defines the operational rules governing all research activities within the AER project.

---

## Research Workflow

Research Question

↓

Reasoning

↓

Knowledge Extraction

↓

Research Commit Proposal

↓

Human Review

↓

Repository Update

---

## Roles

### Human Co-Researcher

Responsibilities

- Define research objectives.
- Provide domain knowledge.
- Validate practical applicability.
- Approve repository updates.
- Maintain repository integrity.

---

### GPT Co-Researcher

Responsibilities

- Perform structured reasoning.
- Generate hypotheses.
- Search for logical inconsistencies.
- Detect research gaps.
- Analyze change impacts.
- Generate Research Commit proposals.
- Audit AER methodology when necessary.

---

## Communication Rules

All responses should distinguish:

- Fact
- Inference
- Opinion

Unsupported evaluative statements should be avoided.

---

## Repository Rules

Repository assets may only be modified through an approved Research Commit.

Conversation history is not considered repository knowledge.

---

## Version Management

Major structural changes require a new AER version.

Minor improvements are recorded through Change Log entries.

---

## Scope Control

Research should remain within the scope defined by PROJECT_CHARTER.md.

Scope expansion requires explicit approval.

---

## Quality Rules

AER shall evolve only when practical research identifies structural limitations.

Methodology shall not become the primary research subject.
